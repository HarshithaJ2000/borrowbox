Section 05 — KPI 6: Overdue Alert Response Rate
New KPI — Introduced by QR Solution
Overdue Alert Response Rate
Measures the percentage of patrons with overdue items who return the item within 48 hours of receiving an automated notification. This KPI has no baseline value because no automated alert system existed before the QR implementation. It is tracked exclusively in Dataset 2.
Alert Response Rate = ( AR ÷ AS ) × 100
AR = number of overdue alerts where patron returned item within 48 hours
AS = total overdue alerts dispatched in the period
No System
Baseline (Dataset 1)
No automated overdue alerts existed. Staff had no mechanism to systematically notify patrons when items were overdue. Items remained unreturned for an average of 63.3 days before discovery.
81.4%
Achieved (Dataset 2)
96 of 118 overdue alerts resulted in patron returning the item within 48 hours of notification dispatch.
80%
Project Target
Target set at 80% of patrons responding within 48 hours. Dataset 2 achieves 81.4%, exceeding the target by 1.4 percentage points.
Why This KPI Is Critical
Recovers items before they become losses
A high response rate means overdue items are returned before the recovery window closes. Each alert-driven return avoids a replacement cost averaging $140 per item.
Directly reduces Average Days Overdue
KPI 6 and KPI 4 (Average Days Overdue) are directly linked. When patrons respond to alerts within 48 hours, average overdue days drops from 63.3 days to 4.5 days — an 93% reduction.
Validates the QR alert mechanism
This KPI evaluates whether the automated notification component of the QR system is functioning. A low rate would indicate patrons are ignoring alerts or contact details are outdated, triggering a system review.
Alert Response Rate Over Time (Post-Implementation, Dataset 2)
Months 1–2: Pre-go-live (no alerts). Month 3 onwards: QR system active. 80% target line shown.
The alert response rate climbed steadily after go-live in Month 3, reaching 81.4% by Month 12 — above the 80% target. Items not returned despite two alerts are escalated to hub managers for direct follow-up. Patrons with repeated non-responses are flagged for account review.
